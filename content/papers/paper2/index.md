##### Abstract

This research investigates the psychological mechanisms influencing tourists' revisit intention to the "Snow Ruyi" Winter Olympic venue through the lens of immersion and place attachment. Data collected from 433 visitors show that immersion significantly enhances place attachment, which in turn increases the likelihood of revisit intention. The study also explores generational differences, finding that there are no significant variations across Generations X, Y, and Z in their emotional and behavioral responses. This paper contributes to the understanding of Winter Olympic legacy tourism by applying the cognitive-affective-conative (CAC) model to the unique context of Olympic venues, offering both theoretical and practical insights for venue managers to enhance visitor engagement and long-term venue utilization.
