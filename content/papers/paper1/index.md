##### Abstract

This study explores the spillover effect of Chinese domestic sportswear brands' relationship quality, focusing on the psychological mechanisms behind the shift in Chinese consumers' preference from foreign to domestic brands. Using a brand relationship quality scale based on Confucian yuanfen culture, the study surveyed 326 responses and employed SmartPLS 4.0 for hypothesis testing. The findings reveal that dimensions such as interaction belief, intimate interaction, and happiness drive the shift, with product origin image mediating the relationship. However, consumer xenocentrism does not moderate this effect. The study contributes to understanding brand relationships in sports marketing, suggesting co-opetition as a potential strategy for domestic brands.
